Database Queries (SQL)¶

Let's start by aggregating wins and losses per opponent for the 2022 and 2023 seasons, we can use the following SQL query to:

  • combine data from the two datasets mets_2022 and mets_2023;
  • group the combined data based on opponent teams and whether the Mets won or lost;
  • order the data alphabetically by team and display wins before losses.

Example SQL query¶

SELECT mets.Opp AS "Opponent", mets.W/L AS "Win (W) or loss (L)", count(*) AS "Count" FROM (SELECT * FROM mets_2022 UNION ALL SELECT * FROM mets_2023) AS mets GROUP BY mets.Opp, mets.W/L ORDER BY mets.Opp, mets.W/L DESC;

It looks like the New York Yankees had the highest average attendance over 2022 and 2023 with an average of 45,645 tickets sold, relatively consistently. This is most likely due to being a local team, attracting supporters of both the Mets and the Yankees.

The Los Angeles Dodgers were a close second, selling 44,107 tickets on average, but with a far more variable attendance, even though they played more games against the Mets. This variability could be due multiple factors, including the distance between the two home stadiums, winning streaks, time of day, and a game's championship leverage index (i.e., the importance of a game on the New York Mets' probability of winning the World Series). We will look into the effects of these variables in further detail later on.

Games against the Toronto Blue Jays were third highest in average attendance with a consistent turnout of around 40,837, despite the lowest number of games played.

Past third place, attendance is similar for the remaining top ten, hovering around 35,000 tickets sold.

Then we can train a regression model with attendance as the outcome and the following variables as predictors:

  • Whether a game was played at home or away;
  • Whether a game was played during the day or the night;
  • The Mets' ranking;
  • The game's Championship Leverage Index;
  • The status of the Mets' winning or losing streak;
  • The Mets' win/loss ratio on the day of the game;
  • The game number.

As we saw in the panel plot above, away games appear to have worse attendance than home games, and the time of day does not affect attendance. The Mets' win/loss ratio also does not effect attendance to games. There is a potential link between game number and attendance, in that attendance may decrease as the season progresses (though this may be because the Mets' performance decreased as the season progressed).

Interestingly, though they do not produce significant effects, the Mets' rank, the status of their winning streak, and a game's cLI show a trend towards being linked with attendance. Given that they similarly measure the Mets' performance over time, they are most likely collinear and therefore cannibalizing each other's effects on attendance. Furthermore, attendance is potentially inversely linked with the status of the Mets' streak, indicating that fans could be more likely to attend if the Mets are on a losing streak.

Based on this analysis, the outcomes of the least popular games of 2022 and 2023 become more clear given that they were all away games and they mostly occurred later in the season. In the future, it would be of interest to map out the weather during each game and determine whether this had a consistent effect on attendance.

Conclusions¶

Both Mets performance and game attendance appear to drop when games are played away from home. This could be an area for the Mets' ticket sales department to focus on, either marketing these games more heavily or reducing prices to increase attendance.

Games with the New York Yankees, Los Angeles Dodgers and the Toronto Blue Jays can also be promoted more heavily to leverage the popularity of these games. Pricing for games should be reduced when weather is expected to be rainy, as this could avoid loss of attendance due to bad weather.

The New York Mets marketing and sales team should also meet with the Oakload Athletics team to discuss restrategizing their approach to generating ticket sales, as they are not performing as well as other matchups.

In the future, it would be interesting to investigate the effects of weekdays versus weekends, or whether specific days of the week affect attendance and team performance. It would also be helpful to know how the ticket prices themselves affect attendance, as there may be a cost/benefit analysis to be done when setting prices.
