Pharmacological fMRI experiment

Project: Pharmacological fMRI experiment conducted on healthy human volunteers.

Abstract: In this experiment that I designed and led between 2019-2022, we examined how a targeted pharmacological manipulation of learning would impact pain perception and their brain correlates. Participants received either a placebo or a single 80 mg dose of dcycloserine (a partial NMDA receptor agonist) and underwent fMRI while receiving painful stimulations to the arm.

Data: This demo includes behavioral neuroscience, pharmacological, and neuroimaging (fMRI) data obtained from 50 human participants.

This project was published as: A randomized pharmacological fMRI trial investigating dcycloserine and brain plasticity mechanisms in learned pain responses.

Please note that this notebook contains only a small example from the analyses performed for this project. The purpose of this notebook is to demonstrate the thought process and types of analyses performed and is not exhaustive or complete. The full analyses performed for each project are reported in the published article.

Conclusions
